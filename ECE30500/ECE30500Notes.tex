\section{Introduction}
The core of semiconductors lies in the silicon transistor. But, why silicon (\textbf{Si})?\\
\begin{itemize}
    \item Si is the second most common element on Earth.
    \item It is easily purified, and grown defect free.
    \item Reasonably good electronic properties
    \item Resilient to harsh environments
    \item Excellent mechanical properties
\end{itemize}
$\text{SiO}_2$ is a remarkable material because:
\begin{itemize}
    \item It interfaces very well with Si
    \item Excellent insulator
    \item Excellent barrier against impurity diffusion
    \item High etch selectivity to Si
\end{itemize}
